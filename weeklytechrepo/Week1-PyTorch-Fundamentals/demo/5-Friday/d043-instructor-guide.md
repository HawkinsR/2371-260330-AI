# Demo: Evaluation and TensorBoard Integration

## Discussion Topics

Use these prompts to check student understanding before diving into the demo. Give students 30–60 seconds to think before calling on someone.

| Term | Prompt |
|---|---|
| **True/False Positives and Negatives** | *"A hospital AI flags 50 patients as having cancer. 40 of them actually do. Of the 10 remaining actual cancer patients, 3 were missed. What type of error is each case?"* |
| **Precision vs. Recall** | *"Which metric matters more for a spam filter — being precise (not blocking real emails) or having recall (catching every spam)? What about a cancer screening system?"* |
| **F1 Score / Harmonic Mean** | *"Why can't we just average Precision and Recall? What would happen to the average if a model had 100% Precision but 0% Recall?"* |
| **AUC-ROC** | *"If you had to rank 10 models by how well they separate cats from dogs, what single number would you want to compare?"* |
| **Convergence** | *"If you're stirring sugar into coffee and the taste stops changing after 30 stirs, has the sugar 'converged'? What does that mean for a model's training?"* |

## Phase 1: The Concept (Whiteboard/Diagram)

**Time:** 10 mins

1. Open `diagrams/tensorboard-logging-flow.mermaid`.
2. Trace the data flow:
   - "Yesterday, we calculated `train_loss` and `val_loss` and printed them to the little text console. As models take 5 days to train, printing to the console is useless."
   - Follow the dotted lines from the Loop to the `SummaryWriter`. The `SummaryWriter` is our bridge. It intercepts our metrics and physically writes them to a `/runs/` folder event database on disk.
   - We then open a second terminal completely separate from Python, launch the `TensorBoard Server`, and it reads that `/runs/` folder, hosting an interactive graphing website on port `localhost:6006`.

## Phase 2: The Code (Live Implementation)

**Time:** 30 mins

1. Open `code/d043-tensorboard-integration.py`.
2. **Setup and Initialization:**
   - Walk through line 4: `from torch.utils.tensorboard import SummaryWriter`. It is built natively into PyTorch.
   - Line 25: Instantiate the `writer`. Emphasize the `log_dir`. "If you don't name your experiments, TensorBoard becomes a chaotic mess of timestamps. Always define clear `log_dir` folders like `runs/model_v1_adam_lr_0.01`."
   - Show `writer.add_graph()`. This takes a dummy input and draws out our network visually.
3. **The Logging Mechanisms:**
   - Find lines 48-55: `writer.add_scalar('Loss/Train', loss.item(), epoch)`.
   - Explain the structure: `Chart_Title`, `Y_axis_value`, `X_axis_value`. The `/` in the title automatically groups the charts in the UI! (e.g. A "Loss" umbrella group containing "Train" and "Validation" graphs).
4. **Execution and Visualization:**
   - Run the script. Wait for it to finish and point out the new `runs/demo_experiment_1` folder in the directory tree.
   - Open a live terminal session. Type: `tensorboard --logdir=runs`.
   - Open a browser and navigate to `http://localhost:6006`. Show the live dynamic dashboards, adjusting the smoothing curve of the Loss and Accuracy charts. Show the network diagram generated by `add_graph`.
